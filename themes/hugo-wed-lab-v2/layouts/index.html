About the Lab

The Women’s Empowerment in Development (WED) Lab is housed at McGill University’s Institute for the Study of International Development (ISID). It is facilitated by Sonia Laszlo, Lab Program Lead and Director at ISID.

Purpose of the Lab

  • Create Knowledge

    Advance knowledge creation, dissemination and outreach on issues of women’s empowerment in global development.

  • Enable researchers

    Enable researchers, policy-makers and practitioners, including CSOs and NGOs, to address global forms of gender inequality.

  • Understand policy

    Understand how social and economic policy can help in providing real development solutions to eliminate gender inequality.

  • Strengthen the evidence-base

    Strengthen the evidence-base on what works, and does not work, to empower women in developing countries.

  • Eliminate barriers

    Understand and eliminate the barriers that prevent women in low-income countries from reaching their full potential, and from being able to fully engage with the economy, political systems, communities and the environment in which they live.

Ongoing Research

Assessing the Impact of Cash Transfer Programs on Women's Empowerment in Tanzania

This project examines how Conditional Cash Transfers (CCTs) can help poor women overcome economic vulnerabilities, and assess their potential to transform gender relations at home and in the community in Tanzania.

Improving childcare options to create better economic opportunities for women in Nairobi slums

This project is providing evidence on the role of high-quality, affordable daycare in allowing mothers to work in paid employment in poor urban contexts.

The influence of affordable daycare on women’s empowerment in India

This project is evaluating the impact of affordable childcare in rural Rajasthan, India.

GrOW Research Series

The GrOW Research Series is advancing scholarly research on women’s economic empowerment and economic growth in low-income countries.
